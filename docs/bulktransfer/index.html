Bulk-transfer of data

This page contains information about how to transfer large amounts of data to/from CBE

Introduction

For transferring small files to and from CBE, users can use scp or sftp either from the command line or using some GUI applications such as Cyberduck or WinSCP. While the scp and sftp protocol work fine for smaller files ( < 1 GB), they are not an optimal solution for transferring large files ( up to TB). For those transfers we provide a dedicated Globus GridFTP service.
The Globus GridFTP service allows you transfer large files either from your personal machine using the Globus Connect Personal or from another Globus GridFTP server by using a 3rd party copy/transfer.

Currently /scratch on CBE is available on the GridFTP server.
Use GridFTP outside of VPN connections to achieve maximum transfer speeds.

To be able to use the GridFTP service following prerequisites are required:

  1. Your account is enabled for CBE (open a ticket if it isn’t)
  2. A globus.org account (see below how to create one)
  3. The Globus Connect Personal client installed on your laptop/workstation (if you want to transfer data from/to your laptop or workstation)
  4. For 3rd party copy from a remote GridFTP server, you also need an account on the remote GridFTP server (optional)

Documentation regarding the Globus system can be found here: https://docs.globus.org/

Creating a globus.org account

In order to use the Globus system, you need to have an account with globus.org. Open https://www.globus.org in your browser and click on the login button on the top right corner. As you probably don’t have an existing account, it will ask you to create a new account or use an existing organizational account. Globus currently doesn’t support our organizational accounts, so you need to create a new globus.org account.

You can do this in 2 ways:

  1. Implicitly by authenticating with your existing Google or ORCID account
  2. Explicitly by creating a Globus ID account in case you don’t want to link and use your existing Google/ORCID account with your new globus.org account (you can still do this later on)

Once you created your globus.org account, you will receive an email with a link to validate your email account. You need to open the link once in your browser to activate your globus.org account. As soon as this is done you can go back to https://globus.org and click on the login link in the top right corner. If you are not automatically logged in, choose the authentication provider that you used before to create the account (Google/ORCID or Globus ID).
If you want to transfer files from and to your laptop/workstation, continue with the next chapter

Install Globus Connect Personal Client

To be able to transfer data from and to your laptop, you need to install the Globus Connect Personal Client. This client will run in the background similar to the Dropbox, Google Drive client and take care of the actual data transfer. Each Globus Personal Connect client is an endpoint in Globus similar to our GridFTP server.

Multiple endpoints

Currently /scratch on CBE is available on the GridFTP server.

The first time you log into https://app.globus.org/ you will see following screen:

Here you can create a new Globus Connect Personal Client endpoint and download the client. You need two specify the name for your endpoint (i.e. work-mac) and then click on “Generate Setup Key”. This key will be later used to authenticate your client with Globus. Copy it from the popup and keep it safe. You will need it for the client installation (next step). Depending on your operating system, you need to download the corresponding client. See the matching installation guide for more information.

  • Windows: https://docs.globus.org/how-to/globus-connect-personal-windows/
  • Mac: https://docs.globus.org/how-to/globus-connect-personal-mac/
  • Linux: https://docs.globus.org/how-to/globus-connect-personal-linux/

Once the installation is finished your Globus Personal Connect client will appear as an endpoint in the endpoint list:

Additionally on Mac and Windows the Globus Personal Connect Client will be visible in the task bar/menu bar

Transfer data between PC/laptop and CBE/scratch

To transfer data from /scratch to your laptop or from your laptop to /scratch you need to open the Globus file-manager in your browser: https://app.globus.org/file-manager.

Make sure that the Globus Connect Personal Client is running in the background and connected (see screenshot above)

The page is split vertically in two file explorers. Now you need to select the two endpoints, our GridFTP server on the one side and your Globus Personal Connect endpoint (laptop) on the other side. You can do this by clicking on the Collection searchbox. This will show your recently used endpoints.

It is probably empty. Just type in Vienna BioCenter in the textbox next to “Collection” and it should display our GridFTP server “Vienna BioCenter”

Once you select the endpoint, you need to login with your Campus credentials.

After that you will see the directory listing of /scratch. You can navigate into the folders and select files. To transfer files from and to your laptop, you need to select your Globus Personal Connect endpoint on the other side. Again you click on the searchbox next to “Collection” and you can either search for the name of your Globus Personal Connect endpoint or click on the “Your collection tab” where all your Globus Personal Connect Client endpoints should appear.

Once you select it, it shows you the directory listing on your laptop:

To transfer files from your laptop you select the first on the right side and then click on “Transfer/Sync to” and select the folder where you want the file to be transferred to on the left side and then click on the “Start” bottom on the bottom right side. You will see a notification about the started transfer and if you click on “View details” you will see the progress of the transfer.

You can also get there by clicking on the “Activity” tab on the left sidebar. There you can see all your transfers.

Once the transfer is finished you will also receive an email.
Copying files from /scratch to your laptop works the same way.

3rd party transfers

If you have an account on a remote GridFTP server of a different institute/organisation, you can initiate a 3rd party copy between both endpoints. This basically works the same way as transferring files from/to your laptop. Instead of your Personal Connect Endpoint you select the GridFTP endpoint of the other institute/organization where you have to login with the credentials provided to you by the organization/institute. The copying steps are the same as before.

The computer from which you initiated the transfer is not in the data path and can be safely closed shutdown. The copying will continue between the two endpoints.

In the screenshot you can see the file-manager with the Vienna BioCenter GridFTP endpoint opened on one side and the Globus Tutorial Endpoint 1 (public test GridFTP server from Globus) on the other side.
